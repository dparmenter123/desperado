                        Car Numbers U.K. style........

Here in the U.K. car numbers are issued from a central government department
and are for the life of the car. The format for these numbers is ;

        up to July 1983,        X X X   9 9 9 X
                                _ ___   _____ _
                                |  |      |   |
                +---------------+  |      |   +-------------------+
                |              +---+      +-------+               |
                |              |                  |               |
        First letter       area vehicle       2nd part of     Year of
        user with the      was registered     numeric code    registration.
        three digits       ie. county
        form a numeric
        code.

From August this year the format has been reversed to X999 XXX as we have now
been through the alphabet for the year of registration. ( A - 1963 to Y - '83
missing out I,O,Q,U and Z). The new format starts again at 'A'.

After that lengthy explanation of our numbering system here I'm sure that you
will appreciate that humourous or meaningful car numbers are few and far
between. ( also much sought after and highly prized - if you really want a
particular number you've to buy the car....).

Favourite tricks are ;
                        rounding the corners of '5's ( for 'S')
                        filing down '1's ( for 'I's )
                        'losing' the space between letters & digits
                        (or 'misplacing' it)
                        '4's for 'A's
                        etc.

All above practices are of course illegal........

So at last.... numbers seen around,

        BP O1L   -  on a British Petroleum Land Rover

        NEM151S  -  on a Ford Escort

        ISR43L   -  (? ISRAEL ?)

        NOI5E    -  on a Mini

        V FA5T   -  on a Porche

Enough for now - more will follow...
