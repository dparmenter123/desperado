One example of computer communications shorthand has me very confused
in a certain grammatical situation, and I'm wondering how others deal
with it.  I'm positive this isn't nitpicking, because soon enough
email will be as pervasive as the telephone, and we've certainly
strictly codified how to communicate over *that*.  We'll need to do
the same for when email is as common as the telephone.  (OK OK, so
maybe it IS nitpicking.  Either way, it's still damned annoying!!)

The situation is this:

When you are using a smiley-face to end a parenthetical comment, how do
you end the comment, paren-wise?  Do you say:

1.)     rocks (but that's just my opinion :-).  So, anyway,
2.)     rocks (but that's just my opinion :-)).  So, anyway,
3.)     rocks (but that's just my opinion :-) ).  So, anyway,
4.)     rocks (but that's just my opinion (-: ).  So, anyway,
5.)     rocks (but that's just my opinion (-:).  So, anyway,

I personally choose #3 from above.  Giving the smiley a whitespace on
either side seems to set it off without ruining the readability of the
parenthetical comment like #2 does, or without the amibiguity of #1.

#4 and #5 are also more pleasant alternatives, if you're from the camp
that doesn't believe that there is a true polarity for the smiley,
namely  ":-)".  I am not one of those heathens, myself.
