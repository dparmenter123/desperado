I am reminded of a wonderful, strange old man I had the good fortune to know
in the '70's and '80's, Cutler DeLong West.  A retired crystallographer, he
devoted his later years to the cause of promoting utility cycling.  He was a
familiar sight around Cambridge, Mass., looking like a turban-less ayatollah
Khomeni, riding one of the fleet of beat-up ladies' three-speeds he
maintained.  To most people, he looked like a wino; he wasn't. I believe he
was one of the original partners of Polaroid, and was well fixed.  He had in
fact established a foundation, which I believe still exists, to promote
utility cycling.  He was cranky, half-deaf, and could be difficult, but he
was an environmental saint.

When I was at the Bicycle Repair Collective, he would come in every couple of
weeks to give us a box of recycled ballpoint pens that he had salvaged from
the roadside and repaired by various means.  When he lit one of his generic
cigarettes, he would split the match in two, so he got 40 lights from a book
of matches.  He did not beleive in wasting resrources.

The reason Eric's posting reminded me of Cutler was that one piece of
equipment that he was never without was the brush half of a broken windshield
snow-scraper/brush, salvaged from the roadside.  When he would come upon a
patch of broken glass in the road (in those pre-bottle bill times, a common
occurence) he would stop, and sweep the glass shards out of the way.  His
efforts probably prevented thousands of flat tires for cyclists who never
gave him a second look on his unfashionable clunker.

I miss him.

Sheldon Brown
Newtonville, Massachusetts

The "dirty words" list (and the eternal question, "What
_is_ dirty, anyway?") came to mind as I was watching "Blazing
Saddles" tonight. Until now, every single time I've seen this
movie on television, it's been censored so badly as to render
half the conversations nonsensical, and remove a large
percentage of the humor - prime examples being the complete
suppression of the soundtrack for the beans-around-the-
campfire scene, suppression of the n-word (and anything else
remotely resembling an epithet), deletion of about half of
Lily von S's song, and even the smothering of her last name
into something like "St...p".

Well, in this night's broadcast, I was delighted (and amused)
to find that almost everything was intact. Oh, there were
still some unfortunate edits to trim the running time - of the
Mongo/Bart battle, only the candygram sequence remained - but,
dialog-wise, this was the most intact TV presentation I've
seen. [I suspect the folks at 56 of having a keen sense of
sarcasm. They did edit out one word with great consistency:
"S2t." And they left that in when it was sung instead of
spoken! Could they have been making a statement of sorts???]
