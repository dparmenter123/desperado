One important point everyone seems to have missed so far concerning the call
letters assigned to commercial radio stations:  The FCC decided a LONG time
ago that all such were to be four letters long.  Existing three-letter call
signs were "grandfathered".  I don't know exactly when this happened, or even
if it happened before, after, or at the same time as the "W is East, K is West"
decision, but you'll notice the large number (relatively) of 3-letter call
signs among the "out of position" stations.

Now that we have THAT over with, here is a riddle:  How are phone area codes
assigned?  Look at the map, and notice the apparently random pattern.  Then
look more closely...  I'll provide the answer in a little while if no one
else finds it.
