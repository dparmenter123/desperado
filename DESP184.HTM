Here's a summary of what happened, from a friend of mine at Lotus.

friend.)  One of us heard some coverage on WBUR radio this morning,

Subject: Tempest in a Tea Pot
Date: Wed, 24 May 89 17:44:53 EDT

Or, at least that is what it looked like from the sixth floor.

About 50 or so very nerdy looking guys with a variety of signs - chanting was
audible on 6.  Some cameras presumably from newspapers, no video that I could
see.  Patrick was the only "normal" or identifable person from my vantage point.

Reaction here was primarily how pathetically scruffy they looked.  A sign that
said "Nerd Power" got quite a few chuckles...

Frankly, they would have had a much better time yesterday when the weather was
fine and TONS of people were out walking on the river.  But, they seemed to be
good natured (kept waving to us) and their signs certainly showed a sense of
humor.

So, that's it on assault # 1.

I just gave a final to a class at Columbia.

The real questions added up to 99 points total;
so for 1 extra point, I asked them to

State the top 10 reasons that theoretical computer science
is important in your daily life.

Here were some of the better answers (note: unfortunately, some
of the answers to the real questions were a lot more hilarious,
but propriety forbids my sharing them with you):

(1) No expensive hardware is needed.

(2) If you've got DTIME, we've got DBEER.

(3) We've got DTIME, if you've got DMONEY.

(4) I now have more acronyms to use with my IBM friends.

(5) No eye-strain is incurred from VDT's.

(6) It makes visiting the dentist enjoyable and watching grass
grow interesting.

(7) It almost makes me believe that computer science is a science.

(8) If you have a car accident and become a quadraplegic,
you can still do theoretical computer science.

(9) I have gained insight into how Fermat felt,
because I have a neat proof that P=NP, but I cannot fit
it into this blue book.

(10) Now I can b.s. my boss about why I'm not writing more code.

(11) I am convinced that the OPTIMAL MAYORAL CANDIDATE problem
is NP-complete and thus do not have to decide whom to vote for.

(12) It helps me convince people that my indecisiveness is
really Nondeterminism, which sounds like a much more
positive characteristic.

(13) It makes me realize how much computational complexity I
lost when I dropped acid.

(14) I have learned that it is possible to get a CS degree without
writing major amounts of code.

(15) (From an OR student who is trying to get a job at Bell Labs)
It makes me feel like a computer scientist.
It makes me feel more like a computer scientist.
It makes me feel more and more like a computer scientist.
It makes me feel more and more and more like a computer scientist.
..
It makes me feel (more and)^8 more like a computer scientist.
