ranges from Leonard Bernstein  to the Cleveland string section to

tell me to visit NET.MUSIC -- opera is a lot more than music, and
someone capable of lumping them together is not capable of generating
the kind of specific endless connoisseur CHITCHAT I miss. Is there

decade? Who loves to hear all the new contralto Rosinas? Who stays

                            OHM'S KLUDGE

||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||

From: PHENIX::SINKEL
To:   KERMIT::PARMENTER

        Ohm's law is one of my most favorite laws. It basically goes something
like this. It states, of course, that current through a device is proportional
to the voltage across it. Unfortunately, there are lots of exceptions, even
among very ordinary devices--like a tungsten wire or wire spun on a bobbin.
The discrepancy from Ohm's law becomes even more significant for  solid state
devices like diodes.

        However the elegance of the inherent proportionality of voltage to
current is so appealing that we don't want to tamper with the basic law itself.
Instead, the discrepancy is resolved in a different way--by introducing the
property of resistance. We say that the current is indeed proportional to the
voltage for a constant resistance, however some devices have the curious
property of variable resistance. In effect, it is not that current and voltage
are not proportional, it's that the constant of proportionality changes--
sometimes, in fact, the "constant" is a function of current or voltage. The
devices are then said to be "non-ohmic". Naturally Ohm's law could not be
expected to hold for non-ohmic devices!

From: PHILPOTT
To:   PARMENTER

For other nodes to get at files on KERMIT, they will have to have some
account/password to get in.  The mechanism that is used is for the
remote user to use the account DECNET with the password KERMIT.  This
account cannot be logged in to from a terminal, so there is no need to
worry about that.  For our group's network, we have "global aliases"
defined so people don't have to explicitly include the access control
string when going from, say, CASTOR to KERMIT.  On the VMS systems, we
have system-wide logical names defined that have the access control.

For an RSX user to get at your files, the must use:

        NFT TI:=KERMIT/DECNET/KERMIT::dduuu:[ggg,mmm]filename.ext

For a VMS user they need to use:

        TYPE KERMIT"DECNET KERMIT"::dduuu:[ggg,mmm]filename.ext

rob.
