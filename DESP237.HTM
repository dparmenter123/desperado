        My major point of sympathy with Ross Perot is his irritation with
        the press for lumbering him with the name "H. Ross Perot" which he
        has never used in his entire life.

        The US Army simply will not accept it if you have no middle
        initial.  Even if your legal name is Joe Turandot, the Army can't
        stand it.  They cope with the crisis of no middle initial by
        giving you *three* of the damned things, to wit, Joe Turandot
        becomes Joe NMI Turandot.

        crooks from the desk sergeant that they now insist on
        birth-certificate names from everybody.  If H. Ross Perot thinks
        he can get away with calling himself Ross Perot, he's got another
        think coming.

        Just the Fax
        by Garry Trudeau

A few years back, ex-Congressional aide Christopher Matthews set
forth a political stratagem he called "hanging a lantern on your
problem," by which he meant pre-emptively establishing the terms
under which you would be judged.

This is what is happening in Dallas, where can-do un-candidate
Ross Perot has been overseeing the lighting of his towering
ignorance.  Recently emerging from five minutes of seclusion and
study, Perot has now declared that the traditional means of
measuring intentions -- thoughtful positions -- are the sullied
trappings of a discredited profession.  Hereafter, Perot informs
us, only his principles will be made available for public
inspection.

Whew.  Talk about calling off your own final exams.
Self-relieved of the need to know anything, Perot can now
concentrate on the myth-maintenance that is his first love.
This he does primarily by reviewing the neat stack of faxed
press queries that are placed on his desk every day.  Perot
doesn't want to deal with any more trick questions like "how
would reduce the deficit?", so reporters are now required to
transmit their inquiries by fax, with a turn-around time of
about 72 hours.

This cumbersome procedure is infuriating to those of us who
prefer dairy-fresh information about the Perot principles -- and
don't want to wait three long news cycles to get it.  So why not
cut out the middlemen of the press altogether, and access the
Populist Uplinker directly?  The fax form [below] has been
provided for just such purposes: for those still unfamiliar with
the above-referenced principles, a set of starter questions has
been provided.

URGENT                  FAX COMMUNICATION               URGENT

Date:           Time:
To: Ross Perot  Fax #: (214) 716-6558
From:           Fax #:

Message:

Dear Mr. Perot:

  I am an Ordinary Person interested in learning more about your
principles.  I am not interested in your positions, because
Ordinary People don't give a spit about positions until they've
been checked out by world-class experts and presented on
interactive TV.  I have checked below the principles I am most
interested in.  I understand that I will receive a refreshingly
candid and colorful response within three business days.

__ What principle was represented by your three different
explanations of why you tried to avoid completion of your Navy
tour of duty?  Was there a different principle behind each
explanation, or does one principle cover all three?

__ Which of your principles inspired the House Ways and Means
Committee to strike a late-night secret deal giving you a
special $15 million tax break?  What was the relationship of
that principle to concurrent campaign contributions?

__ When you lost a Texas Medicaid contract in 1980, which
principle inspired you to dig up enough dirt on the winning firm
to have its bid overturned?

__ Your reported suggestion that minority neighborhoods be
cordoned off for house-to-house drug and gun searches is
representative of which principle?

__ Your lawyer's efforts to subvert the workings of a Texas
legislative committee to win approval of a huge bond issue
beneficial to your family's holdings is evidence of what
principle?

__ Your claim to have compromising photos of a newspaper
employee was prompted by what principle?

__ Your claim to have compromising photos of a Pentagon official
was prompted by what principle?

__ Which principle motivated your recent threat to make
embarrassing disclosures about the Nixon and Republican
Administrations if G.O.P. officials continued making
unflattering revelations about you?

__ Yes, I'm interested in receiving additional information
about your principles.  I have you assurance that no salesman
will call.

This might be the largest that SRL was created, but it infinitely smaller
than the largest walking machine ever made.  The credit for that goes to
Bucyrus-Erie, for their walking dragline, "Big Muskie", residing somewhere
in the spoilbanks south of Columbus, Ohio.

The 2nd largest (there are two) is the BE3270W.  The 3270s reside in
Warrick County, Indiana (AMAX's Ayrshire Mine) and near Marion, Illinois
(AMAX's Delta Mine).  The have walking pontoons approx. 80' long, and the
machine rests on a "tub", a flat-bottomed round structure about 80' in
diameter.

I couldn't venture a guess at the tonnage of these babies, but the 3270's
boom (that crane-like structure on the front) measures 330' long.  The
machine house is about 120' x 120' x about 4 stories tall, and the whole
thing rotates completely in less than a minute.  The bucket capacity on
the 3270 is 176 cubic yards, enough to hold a good-sized party in.

From: sfisher@wsl.dec.com (Scott Fisher)
Subject: Re: US V8 engine id numbers
Date: Tue, 10 Jul 90 22:24:51 GMT

In article <180003@hpbbrd.HP.COM> gary@hpbbrd.HP.COM (Gary Tuosto) writes:
>
>  Is it true that the id is stamped somewhere on the block? I have
>  yet to find anything on my 350. Where is it? How does it break
>  down?

The id of most American V8s is an integral part of  the casting, applied
while the metal is still in a  molten state.  The superego is rarely used
these days as a cost-cutting measure; it used to be applied as a hot-vapor
coating just after the casting molds had been removed from the block
(though Hudson had experimented with brushing the superego directly into
the casting cores and pouring molten iron into the hollows; this was one
of the keys to their  "twin-H-power" engines having such reliability,
because the innate sense of moral obligation provided by the superego kept
them working beyond the realm of most contemporary psychoplants).

The roller-lifter ids in use on modern Chevy V8s are not compatible with
the solid-lifter ids in use  during the Fifties and early Sixties.  Later
Tonawanda blocks (recognizable by a difficulty in approaching ethical
questions without some form of affective neurosis) can use the modern
roller-lifter id due to an effective ego structure in the rocker valley
that enables strong decision-making, effective value judgments and good
low-end torque.

It breaks down first by suffering valve seal deterioration, usually in the
#1 cylinder.  After several thousand miles of this, it's not uncommon for
the Chevy 350 to experience minor personality dissociation, followed by
occasional impotence, anticipatory disaffiliation, and a loss of short-
term memory.  In extreme cases, where the motor has been abused while
young, it can suffer from unresolved Oedipal conflicts resulting in a
tendency for the pistons to gall the cylinder bores.  There has been only
limited success with standard treatment (such as cylinder honing,
dry-sleeving and transactional analysis) but new hope appears to be
offered from a combination of role-playing in a group format and PTFE
treatment of the reciprocating surfaces.

--Scott "Have you driven a Freud lately?" Fisher
