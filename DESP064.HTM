shadowy figure has decreed,

software skills are concentrated in the mail-user area and who was the
victim of one letter-bomb too many.  Or, maybe one of those economical
managers still using the flutter-bell  VT52 who hates ending up in
in-the-weeds mode just because some joker wants to hi-lite his memos.
Or, perhaps it's a Desperado who just got tired of typing CTRL/Z and
flicking the on-off switch (the 0/1 switch to all you
internationalizers) to cure some jagged trick of mine.  It doesn't
matter anyway.  From this day forth all escapes shall appear as a
simple $.  There is a way to get the escapes back though.  See next
item.

Programmers (a radically different breed of bitpusher), and Real Millrats, I

course, by no means exhaustive, but it's a start.

Real Engineers write their own utilities with DCL .COM files.

Real Engineers use TECO because they like it.

Real Engineers wear socks that match their shirts.  When they wear shirts.

Real Engineers avoid Spit Brook.

Real Engineers don't care what's at the bottom of the mill pond.

Real Engineers have libraries of chip catalogs in their offices.

Real Engineers have full VMS manual sets, but they're still sealed in plastic.

Real Engineers appreciate Bill the Cat.

Real Engineers have their own espresso machines, but they let somebody else
pay for them.

Real Engineers know where a PDP-8I disk interface mounts.

Real Engineers drive Volvos or Hondas but they don't fix them when they break.

Real Engineers think 'support' and 'testability' are four-letter words.

Real Engineers smoke in no-smoking areas.  After they steal the no-smoking
signs.

Real Engineers buy time with cache.

Real Engineers don't change printer ribbons.  Ever.  They wait for Real
Programmers to do it.

Real Engineers have megabytes of memory but forget their wives' birthdays.

Real Engineers don't like diesels.  They prefer steam.

Real Engineers don't blanch at mention of Tewksbury.  But they don't work
there, either.

Real Engineers make many careful measurements.  Then they go with what feels
right.

Real Engineers aren't touch typists.  And they don't care.

Real Engineers don't build breadboards.  They let the chips fall where they
may.

Real Engineers have wire-wrapped Apples at home.  Or maybe wire-wrapped
PDP-11/04s...
