From:   HUMAN::TINN         "An Abacus on Amphetamines"
To:     KERMIT::PARMENTER
Subj:   The Great Chile Cook-off

I wasn't planning on getting involved in the great chile debate; that's one
game you'll never win.  But after trying some of the recipes from the recent
issues of Desperado, I felt it was my civic duty to enlighten those less
fortunate than I.

I won't attempt to give an exact recipe for my chile; I never measure the
ingredients.  I just toss 'em til it tastes right.  But here's an attempt:

  1.5 lbs chuck cut into .5" cubes
  1 lb ground pork
  1 lb chorico or linguico (hot Portuguese sausage) cut into .5" slices
  3 or 4 onions
  3 cloves garlic
  A handful of chile powder
  Cumin seed
  Oregano
  Salt
  Cayenne Pepper
  4 fresh tomatoes or 2 cans tomato sauce
  15 jalapenos
  pinto beans (optional)

I'm not going to debate the relative merits of beans in chile; if you like 'em,
use 'em.  But use fresh ones.  The night before you plan to make the chile, pour
the bean in a large bowl, cover them with cold water, then add 1 tsp oil,
1 clove minced garlic, and a few chopped jalapenos.  Leave it to soak overnight,
and they'll be ready for use in the morning.

On to the meat of the matter:  you may call me a heretic (or lunatic for that
matter) for putting the hot sausage in chile, but trust me.  Sear it quickly
over a high flame.  Drain and set aside.  Then do the pork, drain and set aside.
Then sear the chuck chunks and drain.  Then throw 'em all into a dutch oven.

In a food processor, throw in 2 onions, 3 cloves of garlic, 2 tomatoes, and
most of the peppers.  Whiz it into a red-green pulp and add it to the meat.
Chop the remaining tomatoes, onions and peppers into little chunks and add to
meat.  Rinse the beans, retaining the peppers and garlic you added.  Throw that
into the meat.  Add the spices til it tastes right.  Bring to a boil, reduce
to a simmer, and cover.  Cook for at least 4 hours, stirring occassionally, and
adding water as necessary.  (But not too much - I like my chile thick.)  After
the chile's been cooked, to adjust the consistency, put 3 Tbsp of flour in a
cup, add enough warm water to turn it into a thick, flowable paste.  Add to the
chile and simmer another hour.

Remember:  the longer chile cooks, the better.  And it's great reheated!

Serve with cheese, nacho chips, and lotsa Dos Equis.

Ah, so they called you about that, did they?  Well, the truth is I was
in the building, I just got misdirected.  I showed up at the Pentagon at
9:45 for the 10:00 presentation where I was supposed to fill in for you.
There was a large line at the door and things were rather chaotic.  The
people in line were the rudest bunch of people I had ever seen,
constantly pushing and shoving, and many of them didn't smell so good.
They would have conversations where three or four of them would start
talking at once, and rather than stopping each would just raise the
level of their voice. I counted at least five who were already drunk at
that hour of the morning. When I got to the front of the line, a woman
in uniform asked, "Are you here for the conference?" and when I said,
"Yes" she pointed me toward a door.

It was just straight-up ten o'clock when I went inside and large, armed
men closed the doors behind me.  I started to get out my slides when a
man up on the raised stage cleared his throat.  He looked like an Air
Force general.  At least he had the blue uniform with lots of decorative
braid and ribbons from shoulder to ankle.  The effect was a little too
garish to see if there were stars on his shoulders or not.  At any rate,
I figured he was going to open the meeting, and would call me up to the
stage later, so I just sat down and listened:

        "Ladies and gentleman, as you are aware things have been unstable
        in the Middle East for some time, and the signs are that there
        will be an upswing in terrorist activities during the coming
        months.  We have reason to believe that the recent airliner
        hijacking is just the first in a number of events planned to keep
        the summer `hot.' Although we have no reason to suspect that acts
        are planned against our citizens directly, there is a
        good chance that in any action Americans may become involved, and
        the world community will look to our nation to see how we handle
        ourselves in the crisis.

        "We are in some respects fortunate that this is the last term for
        our current commander-in-chief.  He cannot run for office again,
        and he is sufficiently old that he will not be seeking employment
        in the private sector.  You might say he has nothing to lose.

        "So we are prepared this summer to strike back for any action
        taken against our citizens, anywhere in the world at a moment's
        notice.

Now, I have to admit, that at this point I was a little unsure how he
was going to swing the topic over to DECforms.  But I figured maybe they
had an application where if there was a terrorist act, some embassy
official would fill in a DECform at his terminal and report it to this
alarming individual for proper action.  Think of it! They'd have to buy
licenses for each embassy, as well as the Pentagon.  Then there'd be the
leveraged sale of AT LEAST microvaxes for each installation.  I was
trying to decide how to best fit my presentation into this introduction,
(and rather wishing they had told us this was how they were going to
lead in) so I missed the introduction of the next individual on stage.
He was a rather menacing fellow wearing the camouflage fatigues that are
so popular in dance clubs, with his face was made up to match.

        "Ladies and g'men," he said, "My name is Lew'ten Cun'l Raight.

        upcoming summah."
