Hank,

Once, a long time ago, a friend of mine from Brooklyn said that
they had a serious problem with rats in his neighborhood. They even
went so far as to place a bounty on the rats' heads. Many people

Subject: DECmaze

From the Wall Street Journal editorial page - 7 Sept 1983

The Micro Marketplace

Ever wonder what those high-tech folks who brought us Pacman do in
their spare time?  Well, it seems they play video games, and sometimes
not just for fun.  We hear, for example, that at Digital, the maker of
the DECmate minicomputers, engineers have come up with DECmaze, a little
in-house game that pits Digital against brands X - Apple, IBM, Wang and
TRS.  The object is for the DEC player to blast away the bad guys before
getting eaten.  Unlike some video games, this one is realistic:  Even
when the DEC warrior zaps the baddies, the competition retreats just
for a little while, eventually coming back for more.  This of course
keeps all the players on their toes.  It's reassuring to see one
industry that not only lives but also plays by the rules of the market.

Anyone know if this is for real?
