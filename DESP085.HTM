From: CASTOR::J_COVERT         "21-Oct-1983 08:46"
To:   PARMENTER
Subject: New Holidays...

Desperado readers in Europe who have followed the news of the
new holiday in the U.S. (and who have always been amazed by the
comparative lack of holidays and vacation in the U.S.) may find
it interesting that (in this Desperado's opinion) Digital will
not pick up this holiday.

Currently the Roxbury (the plant Ronnie visited in Boston) observes
this holiday as the "location choice" holiday instead of Patriot's
Day (which is otherwise only observed in Massachusetts and Maine).

Digital already ignores several Federal holidays: George Washington's
birthday, Columbus Day, and Veterans' Day choosing to do what in most
cases seems more reasonable -- to observe the Friday after Thanks-
giving and, depending on the date of Christmas and New Year's, to
provide a longer weekend around those days.

A comparison of the types of holidays is interesting:  In the U.S.,
Christmas Day is the ONLY religious holiday (and some may argue that
Christmas is not observed as a religious holiday in this country).

In Germany (or actually Bavaria, which has the most), of the fourteen
legal holidays, only three are not religious holidays:  (May Day, Day
of German Unity, and New Year's Day -- which some may argue is a
religious holiday: Feast of the Circumcision).

And as long as we are on the subject of time off -- one wonders when/if
the U.S. will ever come around to allowing the amount of vacation time
which Europeans get.  Most countries provide five to six weeks of
vacation.  France requires DEC to give employees an extra eight
days of vacation over and above the normal 5 1/2 weeks because
employees are required to put in a full 40 hour week, rather than
the 39 hours which is the legal work week.

From: ROYAL::RAVAN
To:   KERMIT::T_PARMENTER
Subject: The Birds

Ever felt vaguely miffed at those folks who park their shiny new cars
in the farthest reaches of the parking lots, placing them diagonally in
the (usually vain) hope that no one will dent them?

Last week I was strolling through the ZK parking lot, enjoying the
autumn weather, when I noticed a commotion at the far end of the lot.
Approaching the scene, I discovered a very large, glossy black crow
having a noisy fight with the sport mirror of a (diagonally-parked)
blue Toyota! He was hanging on to the mirror bracket and flapping
his wings, and making quite a racket; obviously, the intruder in the
mirror was causing him to lose face.

The crow would lose his hold on the mirror every now and then, and would
flap up to the top of the car and try to crane his neck (so to speak) to
see the reflection; he'd hop around on the roof for a while, cursing in
crow, and then he'd go for the mirror again. When I left, he was still
hanging in there, but he was gone when I left the plant that night;
whether he thought he won, or just gave up, I'll never know.

I wonder if crow-claws can scratch a car's finish, and if so, did the
owner realize what had happened - or does he or she think that it was
the work of vandals, their attention drawn to the shiny cars in the
far reaches of the lot ...
