 In my recent stint at Apple, I was amused to be reminded that technophobia
 affects geeks as much as anyone else.  Geeks are terrified of Lisp
 (excepting the 2000 Lisp programmers) because Lisp has parentheses (!) and
 prefix arithmetic notation.  Golly, parentheses are one of the few
 elements that function identically in mathematical and literary contexts,
 and the notion that + is an operator that will take any number of
 arguments just does not seem beyond the capacity of even a geek.  Taken
 together, these hardly seem such burdens as to justify staying with C++.

        Your comments concerning the twisting of bubble wrap
reminded me of our Halloween practices.  My wife, Alicia, usually
takes the kids out to trick-or-treat.  I stay at home to hand out
the treats.  Not wanting to just hand out candy  I decided to put
on a little show of my own.

        For several years I came to the door with a real double
bladed ax with some red substance smeared on the blade and in a
menacing manner asked what they wanted.  It was fun watching the
looks on their faces as they took a couple of steps back.

        A couple of years ago I started answering the door by
opening it and asking them to wait a moment because I was in the
middle of wringing a chickens neck.  I would then turn away from
the door and twist the bubble wrap.

we never got a large number of trick-or-treaters.  The last few
years we have been only getting the neighbors.  Hmmm... ruins all
the fun.  They know I won't actually wring necks, at least not in
front of them, or chop them up.
