From:   EAGLE1::DANTOWITZ "color  18-Jan-1989 1350" 18-JAN-1989 13:54:26.50
To:     CLOSET::T_PARMENTER,DANTOWITZ
Subj:   On the recent virus

What is "random"?

    The recent virus set loose here at DEC used a "random" number
    generator to generate "random" node addresses, but they weren't
    random enough.  I started looking into this because my node was
    getting hit by infected nodes more often that other nodes I knew of
    (quite annoying!)

    It turns out that my node was selected by the "random" generator
    about 23 times more often than a good random number generator.  "Why
    is this so?"

    Basically, what looks random often is not.  The virus read the
    system clock and used the tenth of a second digit and the current
    second.  This may SEEM random, but it's not distributed uniformly.
    It's like using loaded dice or a coin with 2 heads and a tail.

       :-)    :-)    (=)

    So, to those who use random number generators: be wary of random
    number generators that you "whip-up" or inherit.  Library routines
    are often a better choice (and you may still want to check whether a
    particular generator meets your needs).

David
