tasteful ad disclosing that she charges nothing for a preliminary
evaluative interview session.  Well, she tried.  But the phone company

Subject: DESPERADO: E.T. phones home

I saw "E.T." again last night and took careful notes on the "phone
home" sequence.  Here is what *really* happens:

 1. E.T. learns from the kids and the encyclopedia that earth is
    "home."

 2. The next day, while Elliott is in science class (learning how to
    dissect a frog), E.T. drinks his first Coors and then his second.

 3. He then plays with the word game, turns on the TV, and reads the
    funnies.

 4. The phone commercial comes on TV.  E.T. watches and then reads
    "Buck Rogers," from which he gets the idea for the communicator.

 5. In the meantime, Elliott lets all the frogs loose and kisses the
    girl.

 6. E.T. takes the word game apart and then takes it and a bunch of
    other stuff up to Elliott's room.

 7. Then Mom (whose name is Mary) and Gertie come home.  While Mom
    is trying to figure out what happened to the Coors and talking
    to the school about why Elliott would be acting intoxicated,
    Gertie watches a word game on TV.  E.T., who has been hiding
    behind the TV, says "b."  Gertie then prompts him into saying
    "good" and "phone."  She then asks E.T. if he wants to call
    somebody.

 8. Elliott comes home and goes up to his room where Gertie and E.T.
    now are.  E.T. says "Elliott" and then "E.T."  He shows the
    cartoon to the kids and says "phone" and "home."

 9. And THEN he says "E.T. home phone."  Gertie and then Elliott turn
    it around for him by repeating "E.T. phone home," which he then
    says.

10. Then E.T. says "come home" and learns the word "ouch."

Another surprise: I had assumed that E.T. controlled both flying
sequences, but it's really Elliott who does the one at the end.
