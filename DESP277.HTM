Hi Charla,

I think I've got the hang of this web browsing thing now. It's kind of
like finding my glasses when I was looking for my sweatshirt...

Actually, I had a GREAT net surfing experience tonight. I visited
the Galapagos via Cape Verde. Wierd but true. I browsed
http://www.interport.net/~ednorman/cyberguide.html and noticed there was
a link for Cape Verde. Since Nancy;'s ex-landlord and his daughter are
going there soon, I thought I might dig up something useful for them.
I've given up looking for useful stuff for myself and sort of just go with
the flow wherever the web takes me. So I link to the Cape Verde page, which
is pretty much under construction. I notice a link to status of the active
Fago Volcano. Since I am interested in volcanos, I follow the link
and end up on the GVN home page (Global Volcano Network).
Lo and behold, there is a link to eyewitness reports on the Fernandina
eruption! I follow the link and avidly read eyewitness accounts by
volcanologists and Tui de Roy (my heroine - photographer who specializes
in Galapagos wildlife, especially Fernandina). So, when I back out of
that I notice there is also a link to the Charles Darwin Research Station.
How could I not follow it? So like bits of data from Santa Cruz Island
sped towards me at 2400 baud (the modems in the Galapagos are REALLY slow).
I find cool stuff about the land iguana restoration program, and a list
of e-mail addresses (including one of someone I met there), and a list
of job openings (alas, I have no PhD in environmental studies and my Spanish
is pitiful - sigh - one of the jobs sounded perfect except for those little
glitches). I could NOT have found this stuff if I was looking for it!
(BTW, the Ecuador home page did not have a link to the Darwin Center at least
as of last week). This is amazingly fabulous.

Yours in computing,
Janet
