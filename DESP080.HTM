From: EVE::RUZICH

Subject: Gurus and Uncertainty

Why the mention of unified field theory?  Well, you see, before the
Maharishi went off in the wilderness to study with Guru Dev, he was a
physicist.  Also, one of the higher-ups in the organization is a
Princeton physicist, who was head of Maharishi International
University in Iowa to last time I had anything to do with the TM
organization.  (My last contact was about eight years ago.  I have
nothing against the organization, I've just had more pressing business
since, like earning a living and raising a family.)

You would expect someone like the Maharishi to explain the cosmos in
Hindu terms.  This does happen, but with some surprises.  E. g.,
remember Heisenberg's Uncertainty Principle, with particles
disappearing and appearing?  Suppose this happened to a great number
of particles, in a coordinated manner.  The result would be movement
in violation of Newton's ideas: Levitation.

I've only had the required college physics for an engineer, so I
probably didn't ask the right questions when I heard this.  So don't
press me for more details, or try to refute this idea.  I won't argue
it one way or the other.  I just thought that the DESPERADOs might be
interested in some background information on all this.

The TM folks are absolutely serious about their ideas, by the way,
even to the point of trying to prove them in a respectable way.  They
probably still have reprints of the Scientific American article
detailing the effects of Transcendental Meditation on human
physiology, especially blood chemistry. (The effects include lowering
of oxygen consumption to a lever below that of sleep during
meditation, and reducing for hours the amount of a blood chemical
related to stress.)

-Steve
