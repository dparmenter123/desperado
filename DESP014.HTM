From: HYDRA::YANKES
To:   ALIEN::PARMENTER

        I don't know what KDKA and WFAA have in common, but KDKA is
involved in two other bits of radio-trivia...

        1)  KDKA and KQV (another Pittsburgh, Pa. station) have the
distinction of being the only radio stations EAST of the Mississippi
river to start with the letter "K".  Both of these stations recieved
their call letters before the convention of "K" for west of the
Mississippi River and "W" for east of the river came into being. (I
believe there is one station west of the river starting with a "W",
perhaps that is the connection between KDKA and WFAA.  If that is the
answer to the "commonality" question, you are forgiven for not knowing
about KQV - it is just a small local station.)

        2)  WBZ claims to have been the first commercial radio station
in the world.  The truth is that they were second.  KDKA was the first
station and started its commercial broadcasting career with a broadcast
of the Harding-Cox presidential elections.  Sorry 'BZ...

        Speaking of KDKA - I know one of the original engineers that was
in charge of setting up the transmitters for the Harding-Cox broadcast.
(He still lives in Pittsburgh and is a close friend if my family.)  If
you Desperados would like, the next time I talk to him I'll have him
write down some of the stories of what radio was like back then and
send them out over Desperado.

||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||
