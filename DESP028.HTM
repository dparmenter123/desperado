Subject: WMEX

I wasn't listening to WMEX in the late '60s, but I'm not surprised to
hear that it had unusual programming.  It always did.

In the late '40s and early '50s I used to listen to it and visit the
station (on Brookline Ave. near Kenmore Sq.).

I think that all the management cared about was that horse racing
results were accurate and on time -- and that the ethnic programs had
lots of sponsors (Morrison & Schiff brought us the Joseph Tall hour).

Aside from that there seemed to be a lot more leeway for noncommercial
program.

Nat Hentoff got away with at least three such programs:

  The Jazz Album, From Bach to Bartok, and The World in Folksong.

The engineer on Hentoff's programs was usually Arnie Ginsberg.

There was also This is Brown Boston, a program of black community
news before it was fashionable.

Psychiatrist Shep Ginandes used to sing his own songs and play guitar
-- both with a wry wit (In an anarchistic garret, so meager and so
mean, smell the pungent odor of nitro glyc-er-een...).

And at WHDH was Matinee with Bob and Ray plus DEC's own Lew Stone.

"Invention" and "discovery" are always controversial.  Ever hear of
Emil Berliner?  To be credited as the inventor or discoverer, you have
to be a successful business promoter, not just a technical whiz.
America was named after a guy in the map business.  My father's
college friend, John Athanasoff, invented the memory-refreshed
stored-program computer, but World War II got in the way.  Mauchly
corresponded with him intently and intensely about his machine, but in
later life refused to acknowledge any intellectual indebtedness.  A
court later determined that Athanassoff had in fact been the inventor.

From: REX::LEICHTERJ
To:   ALIEN::PARMENTER ! SENT TO HYDRA::HOOD,ALIEN::PARMENTER
Subject: Re:  Information request in Desperado

It's quite easy to execute DCL commands and return afterwards by using
LIB$SPAWN, the callable form of the SPAWN command.  If only one DCL command is
involved, you can pass it in the call; if more than one, you can write a file
and have it executed.  (You could probably make the file a mailbox.)  The
tricky part is to make sure that you assign explicit SYS$INPUT and SYS$OUTPUT
values (analogous to the /INPUT and /OUTPUT qualifiers on SPAWN) if you expect
to execute the program making the call from inside a command procedure.  (If
the current SYS$INPUT and SYS$OUTPUT in the spawnING procedure are process-
permanent files, the spawn will fail.  If the spawned procedure is to talk to
the terminal, bind these files to the translation of SYS$COMMAND.  You cannot
do this in a batch process.  If you don't care where the default I/O goes, just
use _NL: for it.)

There is one problem with this, which may be what you are concerned with:  The
DCL command gets executed in a subprocess, so, for example, you can't use this
to define a symbol in the calling process.  There is no general way to do stuff
in the current process that I know of.  However, just about every DCL command
that would be of interest (i.e. not including, say, FORTRAN, or LINK, which
could as well run in a subprocess) has a "callable" form; look in the Run-Time
Library manual.

Hope this helps.
                                                        -- Jerry
