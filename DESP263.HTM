 ANGRY and HUNGRY

 Millions of hours have been spent searching for the mysterious "third
 word in the English language ending in -gry".  This search is the
 grammatical equivalent of the "mathematical problem" of squaring the
 circle, a quest of no interest to professionals yet of endless
 fascination to amateurs.  Tawdry is the only word in the English
 language ending in -wdry while cwm is the only word in the English
 language ending in -wm.  Incidentally, the Random House Unabridged,
 through the search feature of the CD-ROM version, yields six words
 ending in -gry:  angry, half-angry, hungry, over-angry, and unangry.
 The -gry is no rude Saxon suffix come down to us, but only what
 happens when you add the adjectivizing -y onto a word ending in -ger.
 This has happened only twice.

 To address another feckless quest, there is no rhyme for purple, but
 doorhinge does a pretty good job of rhyming with orange, if you must.

 As for the tagalong vowel "and sometimes y", I submit the following
 masterpiece:

        Pygmy nymphs (by myth) tryst shyly,
        Try dry crypt myrrh, ply nymphs slyly.
