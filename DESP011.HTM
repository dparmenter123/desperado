                DESPERADO PHILOSOPHY EXPOUNDED AGAIN

||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||||
Whereas, this is a short Desperado, and whereas there are a lot of new
subscribers, and whereas there's been some minor confusion, here's yet
another explanation of the life and times of DESPERADO.DIS.

First off, everything in between lines of vertical bars (ASCII octal
code 174) is supplied by the compiler, along with the headlines,
subject lines, and all the pretty decorations.

Except for submissions from node name NEMO::, everything else in
Desperado comes from the person who sent it, although I usually
correct spellings and grammar. Doesn't *anybody* know the difference
between it's and its?

Submissions from NEMO:: are anonymous.  Most are from people who don't
want their names published for whatever reason. Sometimes I decide
that your name should be left off for your own protection.

I don't forward everything that is sent in.  I rarely forward the
heavily forwarded stuff unless it's too good to pass up.  I often cut
submissions down, but I don't put words in your mouth.  I do save
everything interesting that comes my way, whether I forward it or not.
Information on these archives of network life is available on request.

Desperado started as a mailing for RSX writers four years ago.  There
are now about 170 people on the main list -- all by request -- and
an unknown number of forwardees.

Often rowdy, but never raw, when you come down to it, Desperado is
whatever I find amusing at the time.

PS -

Desperado is sent out via NMail, the most efficient and stable of the
various VMS command procedure mail hacks I have used.  NMail has 12
pages of clear documentation, but you don't even need the
documentation to use it once you're set up.  NMail is a
pseudo-store-and-forward system (stash-and-heave) so you should never
miss a Desperado mailing.  See SMAUG::PORTER for more on NMail.

Thanks also to DELPHI::MANN and STAR::GREEK for other hack mailers
from which NMail descended, and also to CASTOR::COVERT for the RSX Mail
that started it all.

Have an ice day.

Tom Parmenter
